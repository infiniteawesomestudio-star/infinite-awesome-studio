    My Background

    I’ve been in benefits since 2004. Over 25 years of watching how this industry works, what breaks and where the manual work piles up.

    I spent fifteen years in the HR and broker world. Consulting, brokerage, advisory work…and everywhere I went, the pattern was identical. Manual tasks. Outdated processes. HR teams buried under documentation with no real way out.

    Then came ten years inside the machinery of a major carrier solving benefits operations and systems problems. I spent my days coding benefit structures, leading UAT for complex implementations and helping clients avoid their biggest pain points by getting ahead of the problem.

    At some point, it clicked. The work wasn’t broken. The systems were.
