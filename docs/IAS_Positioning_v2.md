# IAS Website Positioning v2

**Date:** 2026-05-30
**Status:** Supersedes Sections 1, 5, 6, and parts of 2/7/20 of `IAS_Website_Revamp_Plan.md`.
**Why this exists:** The v1 revamp plan correctly moved IAS off "benefits-tech platform," but it over-corrected. It treats Ty's benefits/HR expertise as a liability to guardrail against, when that expertise is the single most differentiated asset IAS has. v2 keeps the workflow-first commercial direction and puts the expertise back at the center as proof, not apology.

---

## 1. The reframe principle

The v1 plan keeps apologizing for benefits ("build trust without positioning IAS as a benefits consulting firm," "avoid sounding like a benefits consultant"). That instinct mutes the most believable thing Ty has.

Separate three layers and the tension between "general buyer" and "benefits expertise" disappears:

| Layer | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Who you SELL to** | Depends on the engine (see Section 2). Infinite Workflows sells to general small-business owners. |
| **What you PROVE WITH** | Benefits / HR / brokerage. The most jargon-choked, deadline-driven, admin-heavy corner of business. If Ty can systematize that, the buyer's business is easy by comparison. |
| **What you DON'T BECOME** | A benefits consultant. The guardrail stays, but reframed: benefits is proof, not the product. |

The line that ties it together:

> **Benefits is where I'm from. Workflows is what I do for you.**

Anyone can claim "I build AI workflows." Only Ty can credibly say *"I learned to make the unmanageable manageable inside employee benefits, and now I point that skill at your business."* That is the wedge no generic "AI for small business" competitor can copy.

---

## 2. The two-engine, two-audience model

This is the structural shift from v1. IAS runs two commercial engines aimed at two different buyers, plus one public campaign that feeds the second.

### Engine A — Infinite Workflows (sub-brand / product line)
- **Buyer:** general small-business owners, owner-led teams (~5 to 75 people), any industry.
- **Pain:** repeat work trapped in someone's head; the owner is the system.
- **Offer:** the AI Workflow Launchpad and the workflow service ladder.
- **Role of benefits expertise here:** proof and origin story, not the subject. BeneBots appear lower on the page as evidence of the method.

### Engine B — BeneBots / Automation (sub-brand / product line)
- **Buyer:** benefits-adjacent professionals: brokers, HR teams, benefits agencies. Ty's warm network and native language.
- **Offer:** character-led education assets, automation, FAQ/onboarding/training systems, branded content campaigns, workflow education for benefits orgs.
- **Role of benefits expertise here:** central. This is the audience that values it most.

### Campaign — MyBenefitsGuy (public trust engine)
- Public benefits education character. Drives awareness, proves the "make confusing things simple" method, and feeds the Engine B (benefits-adjacent) funnel.

### BeneBots does double duty (capture this so it stays clean)
- **On the homepage / Infinite Workflows path:** BeneBots = *proof of method* for general SMBs. "Watch me make the most confusing topic in business simple. I do the same to your workflows."
- **As its own product line:** BeneBots = *a real automation + education offering* for benefits-adjacent buyers.
- Same asset, two roles depending on who is looking. The homepage shows it as proof; the BeneBots page sells it as a product.

---

## 3. Brand architecture (locked)

Make the parent/child structure explicit and consistent in nav, footer, and naming so the sub-brands read as products, not service headings.

```
Infinite Awesome Studio  (parent / the studio)
├── Infinite Workflows    → workflow product line · general small-biz owners · the front-door paid engine
├── BeneBots / Automation → education + automation product line · benefits-adjacent buyers
└── MyBenefitsGuy         → public campaign · trust engine · feeds the benefits-adjacent funnel
```

Each sub-brand should get its own light identity (lockup, accent, one-line tagline) so it reads as a product line under the IAS umbrella.

## 4. Rewritten copy blocks

### Hero (homepage) — stays universal, credibility lands in the very next beat
Keep the broad hook, then hit the benefits-forged proof immediately instead of waiting five sections.

> **Headline:** AI workflows for small businesses tired of doing everything the hard way.
>
> **Subhead:** Infinite Awesome Studio helps owner-led teams turn repetitive admin work, scattered knowledge, and manual processes into practical AI workflows, prompts, SOPs, and training systems.
>
> **Proof line (raised, sharper):** Forged in 25 years inside benefits and HR, the most paperwork-heavy corner of business there is. If I can make that simple, your business is easy.
>
> **Primary CTA:** Book a Workflow Review
> **Secondary CTA:** Explore Infinite Workflows

### Why Ty — flip from defensive to offensive
v1 headline ("work most people try to avoid") apologizes. Replace with:

> **Headline:** I learned to tame chaos in the most paperwork-heavy corner of business there is.
>
> **Copy:** For 25 years I lived inside benefits, HR, and brokerage. Deadlines, jargon, and the same questions every single week. That world forced me to turn messy, high-stakes work into systems anyone could follow. Now I point that exact skill at the repeat work eating your week.
>
> Keep the credibility bullets from v1 Section 5. Keep the guardrail: this builds trust without turning IAS into a benefits consultancy.

### BeneBots bridge (homepage) — reframe from "personality" to "proof of method"
Keep BeneBots lower in the layout, but change what they prove.

> **Bridge line:** The BeneBots are proof. I turn the most confusing topic in business into something anyone gets. Infinite Workflows does the same to the work clogging your week.

The shovel analogy stays the through-line.

---

## 5. Implementation deltas vs. v1 plan

- **Founder story moves up.** The benefits-forged origin is the differentiator. Surface it in the hero proof line and don't bury "Why Ty" deep in the page.
- **"Start Here" routing splits by engine.** One door for general SMB (→ Infinite Workflows / Workflow Review), one door for benefits-adjacent (→ BeneBots / Automation). The third v1 card ("help explaining complex topics") folds into the benefits-adjacent door.
- **BeneBots page sells a product, not just an asset layer.** It should speak to brokers/HR/agencies directly as the buyer, while still working as proof for general visitors.
- **Tone across the site goes offensive on the expertise.** Stop hedging the benefits background. Lead with it as proof.
- **Guardrails stay.** All v1 Section 14 legal/positioning cleanup still applies. Benefits is proof and general education, never advice.

---

## 6. The one open call

Infinite Workflows targets general small-biz owners (confirmed). Worth watching: the *fastest first clients* are likely benefits-adjacent, because that is the warm network. Don't rebuild Infinite Workflows around them, but make the proof layer loud enough that those buyers self-select into the BeneBots / Automation door without squinting.
