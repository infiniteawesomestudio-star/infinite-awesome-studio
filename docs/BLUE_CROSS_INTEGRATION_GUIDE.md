#### Section 1: The Origin

```
Heading: "How I Got Here"

Content:
I started in benefits in 2013 as a Benefit Analyst at Horizon Blue Cross

Every single day, I:

- Designed benefit structures for complex plans
- Conducted User Acceptance Testing (UAT) for implementations

- Recommended process improvements to senior leadership

Rinse. Repeat. 1,000+ times.
```

#### Section 2: The Pattern I Noticed

```
Heading: "The Problem I Kept Seeing"

Content:
By year 3 at Blue Cross, I recognized a pattern:

Every benefit administrator was doing the same manual work:
- Writing the same benefit letters for the 100th time
- Manually comparing plan options for employees
- Tracking open enrollment communications in Excel
- Reviewing documents for compliance (again)
- Generating stewardship reports (again)
- Answering the same questions (again)

And every time a new system came out, we had to test it. Conduct UAT. 
Make sure it worked. Find the gaps. Recommend fixes. Wait for the next 
version.

This was 2015. It's now 2026. HR teams are *still* doing this manually.

That's when I knew: the problem wasn't systems. The problem was that 
we had the tools, but nobody was teaching people how to use them.
```

#### Section 3: The Transition (Consulting)

```
Heading: "10 Years in the Consulting Trenches"

Content:
After 9 years at Blue Cross, I moved into brokerage and HR consulting. 
I thought I'd see different problems.

I didn't.

The problems were identical. Just at different companies.

- 50-person HR team drowning in COBRA administration
- Mid-market company doing open enrollment with 10-year-old spreadsheets
- Small business owner confused about ACA compliance
- Larger firm with great benefits but no way to educate employees

Same problems. Different zip codes.

But something had changed: AI was now accessible.

ChatGPT. Claude. Copilot. All of them free or <$20/month.

And yet, I kept seeing HR teams doing the work *manually*.
```

#### Section 4: The Realization

```
Heading: "The Shovel Analogy"

Content:
I realized people didn't need new systems. They didn't need BeneBots yet.

They needed to know that the shovels were already in their toolbox.

Copilot can draft your letters. Claude can analyze your plans. ChatGPT 
can write your OE emails. And most people don't even know this.

So I started teaching people how to use the shovels they already had.

And the response was electric. People started saving 5-10 hours per week. 
Immediately. With tools they already had access to.

But here's what I noticed: even after showing people how to automate one 
task with Copilot, they'd come back and say:

"This is great. But can you make it just run... automatically?"

That's when BeneBots became obvious.
```
